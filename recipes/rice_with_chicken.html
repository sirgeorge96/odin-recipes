Rice with Chicken

Ok so for you non-Spanish speakers out there arroz con pollo is Spanish for chicken with rice. You might be picturing some white rice with chicken pieces next to it- and that is definitely not what chicken and rice is in Latin America.

Traditional Latin America arroz con pollo is a gorgeously colored dish that looks similar to Asian fried rice- except with chicken too! We color the rice with achiote and add cooked shredded chicken.

    Ingredients
  • Chicken breast, shredd
  • Chicken broth
  • Olive oil
  • Onion chopped
  • Red pepper chopped
  • Carrots chopper
  • Celery chopped
  • Garlic minced
  • Cilantro (optional)
  • Salsa Alfaro (English Sauce)
    Steps
  1. Get the skillet nice and hot on medium high heat and add about 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil.
  2. Cook the chicken on each side, seasoning well with salt and pepper.
  3. Only flip it once and make sure to wait until the chicken releases itself from the pan.
  4. Once the outside starts to brown nicely, it should be fairly easy to flip. Don’t overcook it. Usually a few minutes on each side is all you need.
  5. After you’ve prepared the chicken, chop the onions, pepper, celery, carrots and mince the garlic.
  6. The next step, sautéing, goes quickly so it’s nice to have everything ready in advance.
  7. Add the chicken broth to a medium saucepan with 1 teaspoon salt and 1 teaspoon extra virgin olive oil. Skip the salt if you’re using prepared broth that is already salty.
  8. Bring to a boil on high heat, then add the rice and stir.
  9. Reduce heat to medium and cook uncovered (it should be at a low boil), stirring only every few minutes.
  10. While the rice is cooking, heat a medium skillet on medium high and add 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil.
  11. Add the onions, sauté for a couple of minutes, then add the pepper and celery.
  12. After a couple of minutes more, add the carrots. Be sure to season everything with salt and pepper.
  13. Once the carrots begin to soften, add the garlic and cook for a minute more.
  14. When the veggies are al dente (cooked but still firm), add the achiote paste.
  15. When the veggies are al dente (cooked but still firm), add the achiote paste.
  16. Then add the shredded chicken and mix.
  17. Next, you’ll add the cooked rice, Salsa Alfaro, and fresh cilantro.
  18. Mix well until everything is coated in the achiote. Season with salt and pepper as needed.
